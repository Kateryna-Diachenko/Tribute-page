Nikolai Amosov

Doctor of Science, Professor was a Soviet and Ukrainian doctor of Russian origin, heart surgeon, inventor, best-selling author, and exercise enthusiast, known for his inventions of several surgical procedures for treating heart defects.


Nikolai Amosov at work -"The meaning of life is to save people. Do complex operations. Develop new, better ones. To die less. Teach other doctors to work honestly. Science, theory - to understand the essence of the matter and benefit. It's my business. I serve them to people ..."

Biography Nikolai`s Amosov.

  • Amosov was born December 6, 1913. in the village Olkhovo (near Cherepovets) of Vologda Governorate, Russia to Russian peasants.

  • In 1939 he graduated from the Arkhangelsk Medical Institute, and in 1940 - with distinction from the All-Union Correspondence Industrial Institute.

  • In 1952, Amosov, as a prominent specialist in thoracic surgery, was invited to the Kiev Institute of Tuberculosis, to guide specially created clinic of thoracic surgery.

  • Amosov was one of the initiators of the widespread introduction into our country surgery for diseases of the lungs, has made a lot of new developments in this problem. His research contributed to improving the treatment of diseases of the lungs. In 1961, Amosov was awarded Lenin Prize for the work of lung surgery.

  • In 1955 he was the first in Ukraine began treatment for heart diseases surgically, in 1958, one was one of the first in the Soviet Union to introduce into the practice the method of artificial blood circulation (in 1963), Amosov was first in the Soviet Union to perform the mistral valve replacement, and in 1965 for the first time in the world he created and introduced into practice the anti-thrombotic heart valves prosthesis.

  • In 1983 Amosov's cardiac surgery clinic was reorganized in Kiev Research Institute of Cardiovascular Surgery and in the Ukrainian Republican cardiovascular surgical center. Each year, the institute fulfilled about 3000 heart operations, including over 1500 - with extra-corporeal blood circulation.

  • 1959 to 1990 he headed the Department of Biological Cybernetics in the Institute of Cybernetics. For his research in the field of Bio-cybernetics in 1978 and 1997 he was awarded the State Prize of Ukraine.

  • In the 1989 Soviet Union legislative election he was elected into the Congress of People's Deputies of the Soviet Union as an independent candidate in Kiev. Amosov believed that the Western society should serve as an ideal for the USSR

  • Amosov is widely known as a writer. His novels and essays "The Thoughts and the Heart", "Notes from the Future", "PPG-2266. Field Surgeon Notes", "The Book of Happiness and Miseries", "Voices of the Times", "Artificial Intelligence", "My Health System" have been repeatedly published in Ukraine and abroad.

  • December 12, 2002 died of a heart attack.

From the memoirs of I.K.Melnikov, who worked at that time as a doctor:
Amosov was a very simple person - without any aplomb or arrogance. When I was working as a doctor and did not know Amosov by sight, one day, leaving the building with medical journals, I ordered him, who stood modestly near the building, to take these journals to the administration of the sanatorium. Amosov silently took these magazines and just as silently took them to their destination. The next day, at a meeting with the head physician, where I and Amosov were present, Nikolai Mikhailovich, as if by the way, remarked: "And me, the professor, has already been made a courier here ..."
